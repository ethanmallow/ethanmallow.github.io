Menomonee Falls is a lovely village in Waukesha County, Wisconsin and a suburb of Milwaukee. Within the county it is the fourth largest community and the largest village in Wisconsin. The US 2010 Census, had the population of Menomonee Falls as 35,626 inhabitants. Menomonee Falls is were Kohls was founded and where their world headquarters are currently located. It is also home to a Harley-Davidson manufacturing plant. The Menomonee Falls School District operates Menomonee Falls High School, North Middle School, Benjamin Franklin Elementary School, Riverside Elementary School, Valley View Elementary School, and Shady Lane Elementary School. Menomonee Falls also has several privates schools including Saint Mary's Catholic School, Calvary Baptist School, Grace Lutheran School, Bethlehem Lutheran Schoo, Zion Lutheran School, Pilgrim Lutheran School, and Aquinas Academy. Menomonee Falls is perfect for those who want to work in Milwaukee, but don't want to live in the city itself. It is home to many parks. A claim to fame for Menomonee Falls, is that famed broadcaster, Bob Uecker, is from Menomonee Falls.
